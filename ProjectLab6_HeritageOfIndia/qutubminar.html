Qutub Minar

Qutub Minar is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Delhi. It is the tallest brick minaret in the world, standing at 73 meters. Construction began in 1192 by Qutb-ud-din Aibak and was completed by his successors.

The minaret is decorated with intricate carvings and verses from the Quran, showcasing a blend of Indo-Islamic architecture. It symbolizes the beginning of Muslim rule in India.
